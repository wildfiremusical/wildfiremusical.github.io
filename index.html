Symbolism of the Show
Names

Nina

Sharing her name with the Incan goddess of fire, Nina's name echoes her fiery personality. Throughout the show, fire is used as an analogy for her emotions.

Jasper

Jasper is the name of a deep red stone. This name symbolises how Nina's new connection holds and nurtures her flame. The stone also has its own meaning: known as the "supreme nurturer", it sustains and supports through times of stress, brings tranquility and wholeness, encourages honesty with one's self, provides courage to assertively tackle problems, and stimulates the imagination and transforms ideas into action. These are all qualities he brings to Nina's life throughout the show, providing support and consolation.

Sylvia

The name Sylvia has Latin roots and means "woods" or "forest". As her best friend, Sylvia has always been the one to rekindle Nina's flame when those around her attempt to stifle it.

Luna and Chandra

Luna means moon in many languages with Latin roots. Chandra shares this meaning in Sanskrit, and is also the name of the Hindu god of the Moon. Nina's sisters are aptly named, eclipsing her in almost every aspect of their lives.

Marion

Marion (Nina's adoptive mother) has Latin, Hebrew and Greek origins, meaning "star of the sea," "beloved," "bitterness," and "wished for child." These attributes align closely with Marion's story, being beloved by those around her, holding undisclosed contempt for what her husband has become and having adopted Nina all those years ago. In addition to this, "Marion" closely resembles the word "Marionette", symbolising the puppet-like control Michael holds over her.

Michael

With Hebrew origins, in the Bible the name Michael belongs to the archangel and leader of heaven's armies. This name has been borne by numerous emporors, kings and saints over the centuries, and symbolises how Michael views himself.

Kallan

Kallan means "flowing water" in Scandiavian, symbolising how he stifled Nina's emotional flame as a young girl.

Phoenix

The name of a legendary bird with roots in ancient mythology, phoenixes are known for being reborn from their own ashes after death. From Nina's perspective, Kallan and Phoenix are one and the same- as if Luna's newfound relationship is a repeat of her relationship with Kallan many years ago in which she was taken advantage of.

Objects

Sylvia's Aventurine Necklace

Aventurine is a powerful stone that encourages emotional healing and self-love, helping to bring harmony and balance to the heart. Its gentle energy soothes emotional wounds, nurtures action and leadership, and provides an opportunity to heal and release self-doubts.
